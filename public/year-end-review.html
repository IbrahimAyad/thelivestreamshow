The Goal Ladder

Keeping Ambition High & Delusion Low

The mistake most people make is trying to put a 10-year dream into a 12-month container. That kills motivation. Instead, use the four-level ladder below.

A goal you can actually hit

Builds credibility with yourself

VS
A dream that makes you feel like you're failing

Creates frustration and quitting

LEVEL 1 — 1-YEAR GOALS Realistic & Measurable

These must be practical, achievable, based on current resources, with clear steps. These are your actual performance metrics for the scorecard.

Practical Achievable Clear Steps Stack Wins
LEVEL 2 — 3-YEAR GOALS Stretch Goals

A bit scary. Require growth. But not unrealistic. These force you to change identity and shape your behavior.

Identity Shift Growth Required Behavior Shaping
LEVEL 3 — 5-YEAR GOALS Transformation Goals

These require new skill levels, major environment changes, new social circles, and new discipline. They create direction as North Star goals.

New Skills Environment Change New Circle North Star
LEVEL 4 — 10-YEAR GOALS Dream Goals

Not meant to be hyper-specific. They define the future version of you. Set your ambition ceiling, keep imagination alive, give your mission meaning.

Vision Legacy Ultimate Self Meaning

Why This Matters for Your Scorecard

You should NOT be scoring yourself based on 10-year dreams. Grade yourself on: 1-Year Execution (Did you hit your 12-month responsibilities?), 3-Year Alignment (Did your actions move you closer?), 5-Year Trajectory (Are your habits evolving?), and 10-Year Congruence (Are your decisions consistent with who you want to become?).
